OSU Senior Project

Sponsored by Analog Device

Tactical radios, surveillance receivers, as well as spectrum analyzers require a high dynamic range in order to resolve a desired signal at low amplitude, among multiple unwanted signals at much higher levels. Traditionally, these combination of performance is only seen in bulky receivers and spectrum analyzers costing in excess of $10,000. With the development of high performance, integrated RF components, the RF front-end of these receivers can be built with just a handful of semiconductor devices on a palm-sized circuit board. This high performance receiver can be packed inside a small, battery-operated, hand-held unit.
